Project Deep Dive

Exploring the core principles and systematic methodology that guided the drone frame's design from concept to completion.

Core Design Principles

Strength-to-Weight Ratio

The frame must be robust enough to handle flight stresses without being heavy, as every gram impacts flight time and payload.

Rigidity & Vibration Dampening

A stiff frame prevents flexing for stable flight, while also dampening motor vibrations to protect sensitive electronics.

Component Integration

The design must provide secure, accessible mounting points for all electronics, motors, and the battery, with consideration for cooling.

Component Explorer

An interactive look at the key components of the drone frame, detailing their design process and specifications.

Central Body / Chassis

The core of the drone, serving as the primary mounting platform for all essential electronics. Its design is critical for housing components securely, providing structural rigidity, and protecting the drone's "brain."

Key Modeling Steps:

  • Started with a base sketch on the top plane to define the main footprint.
  • Used Extrude and Shell features to create a hollow interior with a defined wall thickness.
  • Precisely placed mounting holes for the flight controller and other boards using patterns.
  • Designed robust interfaces for attaching the propeller arms.
  • Applied fillets to all sharp edges to reduce stress concentrations.

Propeller Arms

These arms extend from the central body to hold the motors. Their design is vital for stability, motor spacing, and resisting bending forces during flight. Precision is key to prevent unstable flight characteristics.

Key Specifications & Modeling Steps:

  • Created from a profile sketch with a **6.00mm width** and **3.00mm thickness** as per assignment drawings.
  • Extruded to the required length for proper motor spacing.
  • Added circular cutouts (**20.00mm spacing**) for weight reduction without compromising strength.
  • Applied a **10mm fillet radius** to key corners to enhance durability.
  • Integrated a flat, stable surface at the end for secure motor mounting.

Landing Gear

Provides a stable base for the drone on the ground and absorbs impact during landings. The design must offer sufficient ground clearance to protect the drone's underside and any attached payloads.

Key Modeling Steps:

  • Sketched a profile for the landing leg, ensuring a wide stance for stability.
  • Used Extrude or Sweep features to create the 3D form.
  • Designed secure mounting points for attachment to the main chassis.
  • Added small foot pads at the base for a stable contact point with the ground.
  • Applied fillets to all corners to improve strength and absorb landing forces.

Full Assembly & Mating

The final step where all individually modeled parts are brought together in the CAD environment. This virtual assembly verifies fit, function, and clearances before any physical prototyping.

Key Assembly Techniques:

  • Used **Concentric** mates to align bolt holes between the arms and chassis.
  • Applied **Coincident** mates to ensure faces of components were flush.
  • Utilized the **Circular Pattern** feature to efficiently place all four arms symmetrically.
  • Ran an **Interference Detection** check to identify and correct any part collisions.
  • Created an **Exploded View** to visualize the assembly sequence.

Challenges & Future Scope

Reflecting on the hurdles overcome during the project and exploring exciting possibilities for future enhancements.

Key Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Drawing Interpretation

Accurately translating complex 2D orthographic views into a cohesive 3D model required meticulous cross-referencing to avoid errors.

Solution: A systematic, feature-by-feature breakdown and constant review against all views.

Challenge: Parametric Control

Ensuring sketches were "fully defined" with constraints was crucial to prevent unexpected model changes during iteration.

Solution: A disciplined approach of applying all dimensions and geometric constraints before creating 3D features.

Future Enhancements

1. Structural Analysis (FEA)

Conduct Finite Element Analysis to simulate flight loads and optimize the design for maximum strength with minimum weight.

2. Aerodynamic Optimization

Use CFD simulations to analyze airflow, reduce drag, and improve flight efficiency and endurance.

3. Advanced Materials

Explore the use of specific grades of carbon fiber or aerospace alloys to further improve the strength-to-weight ratio.
